About the Show

Stories from the 413

The 413 is an audio drama set among the fading mill towns, covered bridges, and autumn-lit hills of western Massachusetts — a place where old wounds run deep and small communities carry large secrets.

Each season follows a shifting ensemble of voices bound together by the one thing they share: the 413 area code. Through four seasons, the show traces love, loss, loyalty, and the long echo of letters never sent.

Created in the Berkshires by a human creator with the assistance of creative AI chat tools (Claude, NotebookLM); voiced by ElevenLabs AI voice generation; and produced using xil-pipeline tools with a coding assistant (Claude Code). New episodes released as each season is completed.

How It's Made

The Production Pipeline

Every episode of The 413 is written by a human, then shaped into broadcast audio using a small open-source toolchain called xil-pipeline. It takes a plain-text production script and a cast list and returns a fully mixed, multi-track episode — dialogue, ambience, music, and sound effects assembled automatically from simplified commands.

The stack is intentionally minimal: a markdown script goes in, a podcast-ready MP3 comes out. Voice stems are generated via ElevenLabs; sound design assets live in a shared library so nothing gets generated twice. The whole thing runs locally, costs nothing beyond API credits, and leaves the creative decisions entirely with the author.

If you produce spoken-word audio and want to spend more time writing and less time inside a DAW, xil-pipeline might be worth a look.

Presented By

Berkshire Talking Chronicle

The 413 is produced in association with the Berkshire Talking Chronicle — a radio reading service broadcasting 24/7 on WRRS 104.3 LPFM, serving people who are blind or visually impaired across Berkshire, Franklin, Hampshire, and Hampden Counties in western Massachusetts.

Founded in 1984, the Chronicle brings local news, interviews, fiction, and non-fiction to listeners who cannot access print media. Programming draws from The Berkshire Eagle and a wide range of community voices — including original audio drama.

The Chronicle is part of the Massachusetts Audio Information Network (MAIN) and is sponsored by the Massachusetts Commission for the Blind.
